# SeedVault360 — Lesson Capture Template

Copy the block below into `SeedVault360_Lessons.md` whenever you and Claude learn something worth passing to Dale. Keep it to two minutes — fill what you know, leave the rest, refine later.

**How to trigger it:** any time a recommendation lands, a negotiation resolves, an approach works (or fails), or an AI workflow surprises you, say *"log this as a SeedVault360 lesson."* Claude will draft the block from the conversation; you just sanity-check it.

---

## Fill-in block (copy from here)

```
## Lesson NNN — [one-line title of the lesson]
**Date:** [Month Year]
**Context:** [what you were doing — the product, the deal, the tool, the people involved]

**The situation:**
[What actually happened. The problem, the tension, the decision point. 2–4 sentences.]

**The approach that worked** *(or: The pattern to watch for)*:
- [What you did / what you noticed]
- [Key move or turning point]
- [The thing founders most often get wrong or forget here]

**Reusable principle for SeedVault360:**
[Strip out the specifics. What's the general rule another small business — or the platform itself — should apply? This is the part Dale actually uses. 2–4 sentences.]

**Tags:** [3–6 keywords for search, e.g. pricing, hospitality, AI drafting, negotiation]
```

---

## Quick guidance

- **Lesson number:** next in sequence (the log is at `SeedVault360_Lessons.md` — currently through Lesson 003).
- **Two flavors:** "the approach that worked" for wins, "the pattern to watch for" for cautionary lessons. Pick one.
- **The money line is the Reusable Principle.** Everything above it is context; that line is what ships to Dale and, eventually, into SeedVault360 features.
- **Don't polish.** A rough true lesson beats a clean vague one. Claude can tighten it after.
