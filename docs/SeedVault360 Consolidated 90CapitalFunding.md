## 2. Captured lessons (platform friction points)

These are framed as platform entries — real friction a founder hit, plus the implication for what SeedVault360 should build.

### Lesson 1 — CDFI partner-referral gate
Some CDFI credit-builder products (e.g., **Ascendus Get Ready**) now require a referral from a resource partner — an SBDC, SCORE, bank, or community org — **before** a founder can apply. Founders who apply cold are sent back to find one, losing days.
**Platform implication:** Pre-connect vendors to a resource partner and surface the referral requirement *up front*, so the partner name is in hand before the application starts.

### Lesson 2 — Personal-credit fraud alerts silently block business applications
An old or unrecognized fraud alert (especially a **7-year extended alert**) forces identity verification that stalls or derails a business credit application. It can only be cleared **bureau-by-bureau** — not from a monitoring dashboard. Worse, stale alerts can carry **wrong callback numbers**, so a creditor "verifying" the applicant is calling a stranger.
**Platform implication:** Add a pre-application **credit-file hygiene check** (alerts, freezes, contact info) as a gating step before any capital application.

### Lesson 3 — Charge-off timing risk
A single charge-off posting can drop personal FICO **~70–80 points overnight** and flip a "clean file" eligibility claim **mid-process** — after application prep, before submission.
**Platform implication:** Monitor for negative postings between application prep and submission; **re-validate eligibility claims at submission**, not just at intake.

### Lesson 4 — MCA stacking is a credit trap
Stacking high-APR merchant cash advances (e.g., Square Capital + Giggle + Bitty + yet another) compounds risk and can undermine a credit funding event or lender relationship already in play. Adding a fourth advance to a stack you're trying to get *off* of deepens the problem.
**Principle:** *Pay off existing obligations before applying for traditional credit.* Don't confuse a three-channel debt stack with diversification.

### Lesson 5 — The April-vs-June tax-strategy trap
The tax strategy that's right in **April** (minimizing documented personal income) is the exact thing that **caps borrowing capacity in June.** Personal loans are gated by *documented personal income*; when that's low by design, personal-loan channels underperform.
**Platform implication / principle:** Raise against the entity whose numbers actually qualify. Point the ask at **business** revenue-based channels (revenue-based lines, asset-based inventory financing, CDFI lines) rather than personal-income-gated products.

### Lesson 6 — Deposit consistency is free underwriting fuel
Running **all** revenue visibly through Square and **one** primary business account builds the consistent, steady deposit history that every revenue-based underwriter reads (Square Capital, PayPal Working Capital, DreamSpring, Capital on Tap, etc.). It costs nothing and strengthens every legitimate application already in the pipeline.
**Distinction to keep clean:** Net-30 vendor accounts (Uline, Quill) build the *business credit file* but don't put cash in your pocket — that's a slow background build, not a cash play. Don't conflate the two.

## 3. Supporting principles (already baked into the plan)

- **Clean up personal credit before business credit.**
- **Pay off MCAs before traditional applications.**
- **Founders undersize the ask** — target **3–5x** the survival number, not the survival number itself.
- **Revenue claims must tie to verified Square or bank-deposit data** — never internal planning documents or estimates. (Square summary figures are *gross*; bank deposits are *net* of Square Capital repayments and fees.)
