## 3. LESSONS LEARNED / PLAYBOOK ITEMS CAPTURED FOR DALE

This is the heart of what SeedVault360 is built on — the transferable, small-hospitality-business playbook items surfaced during Ice to Meet You work. Each one was explicitly flagged in-session as worth feeding to Dale. **These are the deliverables Kevin has been accumulating to hand off.**

### 3.1 — Version-Drift Control (source: Garden Gimlet reconciliation, May 31)
**The problem it solves:** A "new" station sheet silently dropped measurements and altered specs versus the master manual — exactly the kind of version-drift that wrecks small-batch consistency.
**The fix that worked:** A single **reconciled source of truth** with **provenance tags on every line** (tagging each value as restored/trial/new so you can always see where it came from).
**Why it's a playbook item:** Small operators without document discipline lose consistency and money to silent spec drift. A source-of-truth-with-provenance system is a clean, transferable process any small hospitality vendor can adopt.

### 3.2 — Cold-Chain + Food-Safety Control (source: Cold brew tea shelf life, June 15)
**The problem it solves:** Low-acid tea/distillate bases carry a real *Clostridium botulinum* hazard; taste can't detect it, and a refractometer can't see pH.
**The controls that worked:**
- **The "one-clock rule"** — the clock starts at brew (Day 0) and does not reset when botanicals are added; everything must be frozen by Day 7.
- **Acidification below pH 4.6** (target 4.2 for margin) as the recognized chemical barrier, measured and logged every batch on a calibrated pH meter.
- **Frozen-only distribution** as the primary safety control.
- **Standard caveat:** internal operational guidance is not a validated HACCP plan — run it past a food-safety auditor or county health department.
**Why it's a playbook item:** Food-safety friction hits *every* small hospitality vendor working with fresh/low-acid product. A clean, plain-language control framework is exactly the kind of friction-remover SeedVault360 exists to package.

### 3.3 — The "Non-Alcoholic Bitters Trap" (source: NA Aperol base review, June 22)
**The problem it solves:** Small N/A beverage makers assume "glycerin-based" bitters means alcohol-free. It does not — e.g., Fee Brothers are glycerin-based but their rhubarb is still ~4.5% ABV.
**The lesson:** Glycerin base ≠ 0.0% ABV, and the **<0.5% ABV labeling line** is a compliance trap that catches a lot of small non-alcoholic beverage makers.
**Why it's a playbook item:** A clean, transferable compliance/labeling gotcha for any small N/A beverage business — the kind of expensive rookie mistake SeedVault360 can help others avoid up front.
