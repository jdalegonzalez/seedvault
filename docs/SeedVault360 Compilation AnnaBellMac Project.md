## 5. Lessons Captured for Dale (platform entries)

These are the concrete, productizable friction points logged during Anna Bell's own capital work — framed explicitly as SeedVault360 platform entries. This is the highest-value SeedVault360 content in the project.

### From the capital-raise / credit work (June 11 session):

**A. CDFI partner-referral gate.**
Some CDFI credit-builder products (e.g., Ascendus "Get Ready") now require a referral from a resource partner — an SBDC, SCORE, bank, or community org — *before* a founder can apply. Founders who apply cold are sent back to find one, losing days.
→ **Platform implication:** pre-connect vendors to a partner and surface the referral requirement up front, so the partner name is in hand before the application starts.

**B. Personal-credit fraud alerts silently block business applications.**
An old or unrecognized fraud alert (especially a 7-year extended alert) forces identity verification that stalls or derails a business credit application. It can only be cleared bureau-by-bureau — not from a monitoring dashboard — and stale alerts can carry wrong callback numbers.
→ **Platform implication:** add a pre-application credit-file hygiene check (alerts, freezes, contact info) as a gating step before any capital application.

**Supporting capital-readiness threads (same session):**
- Clean up personal credit *before* business credit.
- Pay off MCAs (merchant cash advances) *before* traditional loan applications.
- Founders undersize the ask — target **3–5× the survival number**, not the bare minimum.
